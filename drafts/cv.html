Profile

I build small, exact tools — usually one per problem, often in a different language than the last — and I work as the human in the loop on systems that use them: reviewing what language models and agents produce, and making it correct, grounded, and accountable before it ships. The hard part is never the typing; it is deciding precisely what must exist. Spec before code. Fifteen years across machine learning and data science, LLM/agent infrastructure, developer tooling, and formal methods — on GitHub since 2010, with 150+ projects over those fifteen years.

What I do

  • Oversight & adversarial verification — the human in the loop on LLM and agent systems: checking what a model actually produces, not a sample of it; running independent passes prompted to refute rather than confirm; pushing coverage until new findings stop appearing.
  • Applied statistics & machine learning — the quantitative foundation under the rest: time-series and statistical modelling, ML pipelines, and the judgement to know when a result is real rather than an artefact of the method.
  • Grounding & provenance — every claim checked against its source before it ships, and every artifact traceable back to repo, commit, agent, and session.
  • Spec-first tool-building — building the gateways, inference harnesses, and verification flows the review work runs on.
